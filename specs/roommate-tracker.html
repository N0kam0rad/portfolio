Roommate Tracker

A shared-rent tracker that ends the "who owes what" group chat — log every payment, watch balances pay down, and let each roommate see their own.

BuiltMobile web appLocal-firstShareable views
What it is

The 10-second version

A phone-friendly app that tracks each roommate's monthly share (rent + fees + utilities), logs Cash App / Zelle payments, and shows live paydown bars. Each roommate gets a private link to just their balance; the person fronting rent sees it all tick down.

Why I built it

The spark

I was fronting the full rent every month and losing track of who'd actually paid me back. Spreadsheets didn't cut it — I needed something live, on my phone, that I could share.

The problem it solved for me

Stop covering the gap blind

A single source of truth for what each person owes and has paid, so I always know exactly how much I'm out of pocket — without doing the math by hand every two weeks.

The problem it solves for a user

Every shared household

Roommates, couples, families — anyone splitting recurring costs where one person pays and chases the rest. It removes the awkward reminders and the "wait, are we even?" math.

How it works

Under the hood

A single self-contained web app. A monthly view per person (owed vs. paid vs. running balance), tap-to-log payments, paydown progress bars, copy-paste reminders, and shareable ?view= links that open a read-only view of just one roommate's balance. Everything saves on-device.

Where it's going · how it monetizes

The business model

  • Freemium — one household free; multiple households, history, and reminders behind a small subscription.
  • Auto-sync tier — connect a bank/payment feed so payments log themselves (the premium hook).
  • Built-in nudges — automated payment reminders to roommates on the due date.

Next: hosted accounts so the share links travel, and an opt-in payment-feed sync.

What I learned building it

How I directed the AI

Nail the math model first

I didn't start with screens — I made the AI get the split formula exactly right (my %, parking, a fee divided three ways) before building anything. We iterated the numbers in plain English until they balanced, then it built the UI on top of a correct model.

What broke & how I fixed it

The math was quietly wrong

Early versions produced impossible shares (one roommate "owed" a negative amount) because the base rent and fee handling were ambiguous. The fix was slowing down and pinning every number — base rent, parking, fee split — before trusting any total. Garbage in, confident-but-wrong out.

The automation win

Shareable views with zero backend

Each roommate's private balance page is just a URL parameter (?view=K) reading from the same local data — no logins, no server. One app, three personalized views, generated for free.

Tools that did the work

Honest about limits

Claude built it as one HTML file with localStorage. The key lesson: a static app can't watch your bank — so I designed around tap-to-log and a "sync" button that pulls recent payments, rather than pretending it was automatic.
