What it is

The 10-second version

An on-phone automation: dismissing your morning alarm kicks off your ritual (wake → workout → shower → layer perfume) and delivers the day's scent pick — based on the weather — as a notification with the "why," a daily message, and eventually a home-screen widget. It's the daily ritual layer to Aurë's scent wardrobe.

Why I built it

The spark

A wardrobe tells you what you own; it doesn't help you choose at 7 a.m. I wanted the decision made for me, woven into a routine I already do, so fragrance becomes a ritual instead of a guess.

The problem it solved for me

A frictionless morning ritual

One trigger that strings my whole morning together and ends with "wear this today" — no app to open, no decision fatigue.

The problem it solves for a user

Habit + delight, on autopilot

Anyone building a morning routine (and any fragrance lover) gets a gentle, context-aware nudge that makes the routine feel intentional and rewarding — without another app to babysit.

How it works

Under the hood

Built natively with iOS Shortcuts: an alarm-dismissal trigger fires the ritual sequence, pulls the local weather, maps conditions to a scent recommendation, and surfaces it as a notification (with reasoning), a daily text, and a widget. No app store, no backend — it runs entirely on the phone.

Where it's going · how it monetizes

The business model

  • Hook for Aurë — the free daily ritual that drives people into the paid scent-wardrobe app.
  • Premium recs — smarter, personalized scent matching as a subscription feature.
  • Brand partnerships — sponsored "scent of the day" placements with fragrance houses.

Next: fold the recommendation engine into Aurë so the ritual and the wardrobe share one brain.

What I learned building it

How I directed the AI

Design the trigger and the payoff

I led with the two ends — what fires it (alarm dismissal) and what you get (a scent pick with a reason) — and let the steps in between fall out of that. Anchoring on the trigger and the delivery kept the automation from sprawling.

What broke & how I fixed it

The platform fought the widget

iOS Shortcuts can't do everything a real app can — the home-screen widget was the hard part. Instead of forcing it, I shipped the notification + daily message first (which Shortcuts handles cleanly) and treated the widget as "take what we can get for v1." Ship the 90% that works.

The automation win

A real product with no code

Alarm → ritual → weather → scent rec is a genuinely useful daily automation built entirely from native Shortcuts — no app, no server, no cost. Proof that "automation" doesn't always mean "build an app."

Tools that did the work

Use the OS you already have

The phone's own automation layer was the platform. Lesson: before building software, check what the device can already do — sometimes the fastest path to a shipped idea is the tool in your pocket.
