What it is

The product

A clickable, role-switching prototype with a guided client side and an operator side — built to show the full concept before investing in a production backend.

The problem it solves

Clients arrive at aesthetic consultations unsure of what treatment is right, what results are realistic, and what to prioritize. Providers spend the consultation re-educating instead of planning.

What the prototype does

  • Client intake and goal-setting
  • AI-drafted facial assessment
  • Side-by-side treatment comparison
  • Clinic review board
  • Plan output with cost ranges and timelines

Who it's for

  • Medspas and aesthetic clinics
  • Injectors and nurse injectors
  • Estheticians and skincare providers
  • Cosmetic dermatology practices

How I built it

The spark

I wanted a preview, not a filter

I wanted to make a high-trust consultation feel visual, structured, and easier to explain — without pretending the prototype was a production system. Beauty filter tools weren't designed with natural results, facial balancing, or realistic aging prevention in mind.

The build move

Data model first, interface second

I started with the data model: clients, consults, review states, plan items, pricing, confidence notes, and role switching. That gave the interface a skeleton before the screens got polished.

What AI did well

Scaffold and iteration speed

AI helped turn the workflow into a fast React prototype, then helped me iterate on states, labels, and the story the demo needed to tell. The first working version came together in a single session.

What needed human taste

The polish pass

The first UI was too generic. The polish pass added stronger hierarchy, editorial cards, trust language, and a clearer before-demo-after narrative. That's the version worth showing.

What changed

From generic to specific

The first pass felt like any prototype. The stronger version needed a board-style interface, role-switching that felt intentional, and a demo arc that built confidence progressively — not all at once. The narrative of the demo matters as much as the UI.

What's next

Beta validation

Validate the workflow with real medspa providers. Find out what report sections matter most before building the production layer.

Phase 2 build

Real auth, secure photo upload pipeline, clinic admin settings, a stricter review state machine, and report generation with PDF export.

The line between demo and product

Some of what's in the prototype should stay demo-only storytelling. The beta conversations will decide what deserves a production backend and what's best kept as a guided script for the sales moment.
