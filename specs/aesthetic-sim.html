Product Spec · App 03

AestheticSim

An identity-preserving facial-simulation & treatment-planning platform for aesthetic clinics — a client portal and a clinician cockpit in one.

PrototypeB2B SaaSTwo-sidedAI-assisted
What it is

The 10-second version

A web app where a clinic invites a client, the client consents and uploads photos, AI generates a before/after simulation plus a draft treatment plan, and the clinician reviews, edits, and sends it — with identity-preservation guardrails and confidence scoring built in.

Why I built it

The spark

Aesthetic consults run on imagination — clients are asked to trust an outcome they can't see, and providers hand-write plans from scratch every time. I wanted to turn that fuzzy, high-stakes conversation into something visual, structured, and repeatable.

The problem it solved for me

Idea → demoable product

I had a full two-sided platform in my head and no engineering team. Using AI, I took it from concept to a clickable, role-switching prototype I can actually show a clinic — fast enough to test the idea before spending a dollar on development.

The problem it solves for a user

Conversions & clinician time

Clinics lose bookings when clients can't picture (or trust) the result, and clinicians burn hours drafting plans by hand. AestheticSim gives clients a credible, identity-safe before/after and gives clinicians an editable, priced plan in minutes — lifting consult-to-booking conversion while cutting admin time.

How it works

Under the hood

A React prototype with a role "impersonator" to demo every state. The client flow handles consent → guided photo capture → status → before/after slider → treatment plan with live pricing. The clinician flow is a Kanban pipeline (AI Processing → Needs Review → Approved) feeding a dual-pane review cockpit where the AI-drafted analysis and plan are fully editable before sending. Identity-preservation and confidence checks are surfaced as trust signals throughout.

Where it's going · how it monetizes

The business model

A B2B SaaS sold to clinics and med-spas, not consumers:

  • Per-clinic subscription — a monthly base tier by location, with seats for each clinician.
  • Usage tiers — priced by consultation volume, so it scales with the clinic's growth.
  • Add-ons — white-labeled client portal, outcome analytics, and a booking integration as upsells.
  • Wedge — land with the free-feeling "client visualization" hook, expand into the full plan-and-pipeline workflow.

Next steps: validate pricing with 3–5 clinics, then build the real photo pipeline and consent/compliance layer.

What I learned building it

How I directed the AI

Give it the data model first

I handed the AI a complete mock database (clients, consultations, plans, pricing) before any screens. With the data contract fixed up front, the two sides — client app and clinician cockpit — stayed coherent instead of drifting. A built-in role "impersonator" let me demo every state without auth.

What broke & how I fixed it

AI defaults to generic

The first pass looked like a stock CRUD dashboard. I had to explicitly push for clinical-luxe editorial design and trust signals (identity-preservation badges, confidence scores) — the things that make a medical tool feel safe. Specific design direction beat "make it nice" every time.

The automation win

A clickable demo before a backend

The mock-data layer plus the role switcher means I can walk a clinic through the entire flow — today, with zero backend. Validate the idea and the pricing with real buyers before spending a dollar building infrastructure.

Tools that did the work

Gemini → React → polish

Gemini Canvas to scaffold the two-sided prototype fast, React for the interactive build, then a dedicated design-polish pass (editorial type, layered shadows) to take it from "prototype" to "pitch-ready."
