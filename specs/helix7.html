What it is

The 10-second version

A workflow more than an app: I record rambling voice notes about a story, AI transcribes them, then turns the mess into a structured outline, a polished sample opening chapter, and a practical develop-and-publish guide.

Why I built it

The spark

My best story ideas arrive out loud, in the car, at 2 a.m. — and then vanish. I wanted a way to capture them by talking and have the structure appear, instead of facing a blank page.

The problem it solved for me

From scattered to shaped

Turned a folder of disconnected voice memos into an actual manuscript skeleton I can build on — the hardest part of writing (getting from idea-soup to structure) handled.

The problem it solves for a user

Anyone with a story, no time

Aspiring writers, busy founders, anyone with ideas but no hours to draft. Speak it; the AI gives you the bones. A low-friction on-ramp to actually finishing creative work.

How it works

Under the hood

Audio files in → transcription → AI compiles the transcripts into a comprehensive story document (themes, beats, characters) → an outline and a sample chapter draft → a tailored guide for developing and publishing the web novel. A repeatable pipeline I can re-run as I record more.

What I learned building it

How I directed the AI

Capture first, judge later

I let myself ramble in the voice notes with zero structure and told the AI to find the story inside the mess — extract themes and beats before drafting prose. Separating "get it all out" from "shape it" is what made the raw audio usable.

What broke & how I fixed it

Big audio choked it

Transcribing everything at once was slow and flaky. The fix was processing the smaller files first to get momentum, then batching the rest — a staged approach instead of one giant pass. Same lesson as my app builds: phase the work.

The automation win

A repeatable creative pipeline

Audio → transcript → outline → draft → publish-guide is now a pipeline, not a one-off. I can record new notes anytime and run them through the same flow — turning an unreliable burst of inspiration into a reliable process.

Tools that did the work

One model, end to end

Claude handled transcription analysis, structure, and drafting in one place. The insight: for creative work, keeping the whole chain with one model preserves voice and context better than stitching tools together.
