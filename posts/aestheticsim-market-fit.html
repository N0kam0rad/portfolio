Medspa SaaS AI consultation + simulation Pre-validation stage

01 · The Problem

Medspa consultations leave nothing behind.

A client sits through a verbal explanation and maybe a static photo. They leave with no durable record of what was discussed. The provider rebuilds the same explanation, from scratch, for every single consultation.

For the client

No visual proof

They can barely remember what was actually recommended by the time they get to checkout, let alone home.

For the provider

No repeatable system

Every consultation starts at zero — there's no structured intake, no AI-assisted analysis, no plan template to build from.

The actual gap: nobody sells a no-hardware, affordably-priced AI consultation + simulation + treatment-planning tool to the independent medspa that can't justify a $20–40K imaging system.

04 · What's Already Built

This isn't a sketch. It's a working prototype.

  • Real on-device facial analysis (MediaPipe Face Landmarker — 478 3D landmarks, no server, nothing leaves the device by default)
  • Skin scoring across 7 dimensions with a tappable heat-map overlay
  • Identity-preserving on-photo treatment simulation, calibrated to real clinical units — Botox in U per zone, filler in mL per region, Sculptra in vials, all cited to published dosing research
  • Full workflow: client intake → photo capture → provider review queue → auto-generated starter plan → provider customization (treatment library, custom treatments, product recommendations) → approval → client-facing report
  • Provider-side governance: a dose-visibility toggle and a full simulation kill-switch (for jurisdictions that regulate digital before/afters)
Cost today: effectively $0. 100% client-side, hosted free on GitHub Pages. No infrastructure bill is forcing a decision yet.

07 · What's NOT Yet Validated

The honest part.

  • Zero paying customers. Zero confirmed willingness-to-pay. Every number on the last two slides is a model, not a measurement.
  • No real-world clinical/legal review. "Approximate planning visual" disclaimers exist in-app, but no actual liability review has happened for real patient use.
  • State-by-state regulation is unresearched. Several states regulate digitally-simulated before/afters in medical advertising — which states, and how, isn't mapped yet.
  • Solo go-to-market capacity is unproven. A 41,200-operator fragmented market usually needs a sales motion — can one person actually reach and convert independents without a team?
  • Incumbents can move down-market. Crisalix or a well-funded EMR (Aesthetic Record, Boulevard) adding simulation would erase the wedge fast.

11 · Sources & Methodology

Every claim above, traced to where it came from.

Market research disagrees with itself constantly — different firms scope "the market" differently (global vs. US, revenue vs. spend, this year vs. a decade out). Rather than blend those into one clean-sounding number, here's the actual range and the actual source behind every figure used in this analysis.

Market size (footnote 1)

  • Grand View Research — global medspa market: $24.2B (2025) → $27.8B (2026) → $78.2B (2033), 15.9% CAGR. The U.S. holds the largest share of this global figure, but it is not a US-only number. grandviewresearch.com →
  • Precedence Research — US-specific: $7.43B (2025) → $28.80B (2035), 14.51% CAGR. Global figure separately: $25.28B (2025) → $97.91B (2035), 14.5% CAGR. precedenceresearch.com →
  • AmSpa (the trade association, not a research firm) — states current US industry revenue at "$17B+", growing "$1B+/yr." No forward CAGR published on the public page; the detailed ownership/PE breakdown sits behind a $995 report or Plus-membership paywall. americanmedspa.org →

Operator count & fragmentation (footnote 2)

  • Orbital med-spa operator dataset — 42,473 active US operators (2026); ~97% single-location per this dataset. This is the number the SAM calculation on slide 6 is built from. withorbital.com →
  • Other datasets define "independent" differently and land lower — one puts single-owner practices at 60.37% (vs. 39.63% part of a larger brand). Treat 97% as one credible estimate, not a settled fact.

Private-equity consolidation (footnote 3)

  • Aggregated 2026 M&A coverage puts PE-backed ownership at roughly 3% of the total market today, with AmSpa forecasting record roll-up activity this year as PE-backed platforms enter recapitalization cycles. AmSpa M&A outlook → · NERO →
  • An earlier draft of this analysis cited "$3.1B across 400 PE deals" and "0.2% max chain market share" — neither figure could be traced to a public, citable source on re-check, so both were removed rather than left unverified.

Medspa software pricing

  • GlossGenius $24/mo, Vagaro $30/mo, AestheticsPro $160/mo, Mangomint $165/mo, Boulevard $158–369/mo (tiered/per location); before/after photo storage sold separately as a $50–150/mo EMR add-on. Optimantra → · GlossGenius → · Vagaro → · FindEMR →

Go-to-market channels

  • AmSpa membership pricing ($395 Basic / $845 Plus) and AAFE forum pricing ($597/yr, 8,000+ members) confirmed directly from each organization's own membership page — see links on the Go-To-Market slide above.

Clinical grounding & the 3D-simulation precedent

  • Dosing units, treatment longevity, the Crisalix/VECTRA precedent, the simulation-accuracy-correlates-with-satisfaction research, vascular safety zones, and why FLAME/KeenTools were evaluated but not integrated — all fully cited in the build log's Clinical grounding & research section, not repeated here.
The honest summary: the qualitative story here is solid — big, fast-growing, overwhelmingly fragmented, and getting more competitive. The precise dollar figures are not — different sources disagree by 3–4x depending on scope. Build decisions on the fragmentation and the competitive gap, which hold up across every source checked; treat the exact market-size number as directional.
