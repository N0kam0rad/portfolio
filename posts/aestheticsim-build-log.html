How I built AestheticSim

AestheticSim started as a fuzzy consultation-platform idea and became a clickable, role-switching prototype with a client view, a clinic view, mock data, and a demo-ready review board.

ServedReact prototypeMock dataAI-assisted build
Demo video

The short walkthrough shows the working board, analysis panels, review workflow, and plan output.

The spark

I wanted a way to make a high-trust consultation feel visual, structured, and easier to explain without pretending the prototype was a production system.

The build move

I started with the data model: clients, consults, review states, plan items, pricing, confidence notes, and role switching. That gave the interface a skeleton before the screens got pretty.

What AI did well

AI helped turn the workflow into a fast React prototype, then helped me iterate on states, labels, and the story the demo needed to tell.

What needed human taste

The first UI was too generic. The polish pass added stronger hierarchy, editorial cards, trust language, and a clearer before-demo-after narrative.

What I would build next

Real auth, a safer upload pipeline, clinic admin settings, a stricter review state machine, and analytics that show where the consultation turns into booking intent.
