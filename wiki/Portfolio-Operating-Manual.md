# Portfolio Operating Manual

This portfolio is both a public site and a proof point: it should show the shipped things, the thinking behind them, and the way AI helped turn ideas into demos.

## Core message

AI closes the gap between a rough idea and something people can click.

The site should make visitors understand that Tamara can:

- Identify a real problem
- Shape it into a product concept
- Use AI to prototype quickly
- Direct the taste, UX, copy, and workflow
- Explain the build clearly to technical and nontechnical people

## Tone

The writing should feel:

- Warm
- Casual
- Smart
- Slightly cheeky
- Product-minded
- Honest about what is built, prototyped, testing, or conceptual

Avoid:

- Generic AI language
- Inflated startup claims
- Pretending prototypes are fully scaled products
- Exposing private personal details
- Portfolio filler with no proof

## Design system for portfolio shell

Use the warm editorial system:

- Warm paper background: `#F4EEE2`
- Ink: `#1A1613`
- Red: `#ED3A24`
- 2px black borders
- Hard offset shadows
- Bricolage Grotesque, Fraunces, Inter
- Bold cards, bright tags, tactile editorial layout

## Design system for apps

Apps can have their own product-specific design systems.

Example: AestheticSim uses a cool OS-style palette:

- Body: `#F1F1F5`
- Zinc neutrals
- Violet accents
- Dark `zinc-950` headers

Do not force portfolio beige into app UIs when the app needs a different product mood.

## Gating language

The portfolio is static. Any app gate is a soft gate, not security.

Use:

> Follow or subscribe to unlock the demo link.

Do not use:

> Secure private access.

## Standard workflow for changes

Before pushing:

1. Confirm the correct root path.
2. Check any changed public copy for privacy issues.
3. Use dummy data for sensitive projects.
4. Confirm every image has useful alt text.
5. Confirm app cards with screenshots appear before placeholder cards.
6. Confirm links work relative to the root deployment.

## Good portfolio copy pattern

Each product should be framed like this:

1. I had a problem.
2. I turned it into a product concept.
3. I used AI to prototype it.
4. I made product decisions, not just prompts.
5. Here is what works now.
6. Here is what I would build next.
